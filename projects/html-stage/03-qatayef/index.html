When you say Qatayef to a Lebanese person, they immediately think of Ramadan.
It's the most common, budget, and tasty dessert when filled with Ashta, walnuts or even sweetened white cheese!

Note That the Qatayef is a common recipe in all the Middle East, not just in Lebanon.

Ingredients

Batter

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup fine semolina
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/2 cups warm water (approx)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda

Filling

  • Ashta for dessert (sweet Lebanese clotted cream)
  • Crushed pistachio
  • Sugar syrup for topping (or honey)
  • 2 cups granulated white sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • Squeeze lemon juice

Ashta Ø§Ù„Ù‚Ø´Ø¯Ø© is popular in the Middle East and is common as a topping for desserts like fruit cocktails and as a filling in sweets, such as Qatayef. Think of it like the Middle Eastern version of whipped cream but with the texture of mascarpone cheese.

Cooking Instructions

For Batter

  1. Combine the dry ingredients well in a large bowl.
  2. Start adding water gradually while stirring until everything is well incorporated. You should have a slightly runny but thick texture.
  3. Set aside, cover and let it rest for around 20 to 30 minutes.
    1. Get a non-stick pan.
    2. Brush with a very very thin layer of vegetable oil,
    3. turn the heat to medium,
    4. pour approximately 2 tablespoon of the batter.

For Filling

  1. Add 1 tablespoon of Ashta,
  2. cottage cheese or clotted cream.
  3. Pinch 2/3 of the pancake to seal properly and keep the last part open.
  4. Dip the open side in crushed pistachio

Sugar Syrup

  1. Mix 2 cups of sugar in one cup of water and cook until boiling and the sugar has melted.
  2. Add a squeeze of lemon juice with a few drops of rose water and orange blossom water.
  3. Set aside to cool down so it becomes a bit thick before you drizzle on your dessert.

Serving The Recipe

Drizzle sugar syrup (preferred) or honey on top and enjoy!!
